# Brent Crude Forecast Today: Oil Price Analysis & Key Levels

Brent crude is trading near **$76.01 per barrel** on July 12, 2026, after a volatile week driven by renewed US–Iran tensions and Strait of Hormuz shipping disruptions. For energy traders and macro-focused investors, oil is no longer just a commodity story — it is the transmission mechanism linking geopolitics to inflation expectations and Federal Reserve policy. ## Current Price Action / Market Overview

Brent settled the latest session around **$76.01**, with an intraday range of approximately **$75.31 to $77.56** and a daily change near **+2.5%** relative to the prior close. Front-month Brent remains elevated compared with early-July lows near $72, reflecting a war premium that markets have not fully unwound despite intermittent diplomatic headlines.

The immediate driver is a familiar one in 2026: **Middle East supply anxiety**. Reports of strikes near Iranian infrastructure, disruptions to tanker traffic through Hormuz, and follow-on inflation fears have kept crude bid even when safe-haven flows dominate other assets. At the same time, Friday's partial pullback — WTI and Brent both eased after reports of continued US–Iran technical talks — shows the market is still two-sided. Oil traders are pricing escalation risk, while macro desks are watching whether those same prices feed a September Fed hike narrative.

Cross-asset context matters. Rising crude has contributed to a repricing of rate expectations, with markets assigning a meaningful probability to a September Fed move. That linkage explains why gold softened late last week even as headlines worsened — energy inflation can hurt multiple asset classes at once.

## Technical Analysis

On the daily timeframe, Brent is holding above the **$72–$74 base** built in the first week of July but has struggled to sustain breaks above **$79–$80**, where prior spike highs from July 8–9 created supply. Momentum is mixed: the weekly gain is impressive, but the last two sessions show upper-wick rejection, consistent with profit-taking into the weekend.

The **50-day moving average** on Brent (approximately $73–$74 zone on recent structure) is acting as rising support after July's breakout. RSI on the daily chart is near **58–62**, neither overbought nor oversold — consistent with a market pausing after a sharp geopolitical rally rather than trending cleanly in one direction.

MACD on Brent remains positive on the weekly view, but the daily histogram has flattened, suggesting deceleration. That pattern often precedes either a bull flag continuation or a deeper mean-reversion toward $74 if diplomatic headlines improve.

## Fundamental Factors

The fundamental backdrop for Brent crude today is dominated by **geopolitical supply risk** and **monetary policy sensitivity**. Escalation in the Gulf raises the perceived probability of physical disruption, even when actual export volumes have not collapsed. Traders price the option value of a supply shock, which is why Brent can rally on headlines alone.

Simultaneously, higher oil feeds **inflation expectations**. With US CPI due July 14, 2026, energy pass-through is a first-order concern for the Fed narrative. Minutes from the June FOMC meeting already showed some policymakers favored a hike before the hold decision; firm CPI could revive that debate. Fed Chair testimony the same week adds event risk.

On the supply side, OPEC+ discipline and steady non-OPEC output cap extreme upside unless disruption becomes tangible. On demand, global PMIs remain mixed — enough growth to consume barrels, but not so strong that $80+ Brent looks comfortable for central banks.

## Trading Outlook / Forecast

**Bullish case:** Diplomatic efforts fail to restore Hormuz flows, or a fresh escalation headline hits before CPI. Brent reclaims **$77.50–$78.00** and tests **$79–$80**, with a sustained break opening a move toward the upper $80s if physical supply fears intensify. Inflation breakevens and front-end yields would likely rise in tandem, reinforcing the macro bid.

**Bearish case:** De-escalation headlines gain traction, US–Iran talks progress, or Hormuz traffic normalizes. Brent fades through **$75.30** toward **$74.00–$72.50**, eroding the war premium accumulated since early July. A soft CPI print could accelerate the move as Fed hike odds retreat.

Base case (near term): **$74.50–$78.00 range** into CPI, with headline-driven spikes in both directions. Oil remains a volatility amplifier for the broader market until the inflation data either confirms or rejects the hawkish repricing.

## FAQ

**Why is Brent rising if diplomats are talking?** Markets price tail-risk. Talks reduce the probability of worst-case disruption but do not eliminate it; the residual premium keeps Brent above pre-escalation levels.

**How does Brent affect gold and equities?** Higher Brent can lift inflation expectations and yields, which often pressures gold and compresses equity multiples — even when geopolitical risk is elevated.

**What is the biggest catalyst this week?** US CPI on July 14 and Fed Chair testimony. Energy prices will be scrutinized for second-round inflation effects.
