FXStreet (July 13, 2026) notes traders pricing a **~90% probability of a Fed rate hike by year-end** on CME FedWatch — a combination that typically lifts the US dollar and real yields, capping haven demand for non-yielding gold even when headlines are unsettling.

## Technical Analysis

On the 4-hour and daily timeframe, XAUUSD is **bearish-to-neutral**: price is compressing under the **$4,110–$4,155** supply zone built from recent swing highs, while buyers defend the **$4,052** session low. The structure since the **$4,155.10** daily close (Yahoo OHLC, early July) reflects successive failures at lower highs — consistent with distribution after a multi-month advance, not a fresh impulse leg higher.

According to Vantage Markets' July 13 XAUUSD chart review (TradingView setup), gold was trading **below a declining 50-period moving average near $4,102** and a rolling 200-period average near **$4,081** — both rolling over as price accepts sub-$4,100 trade. MACD on the daily chart has flattened with a negative bias in recent sessions, matching the loss of momentum after July's rejection from **$4,145–$4,155**.

Intraday, Monday's **$4,111.60** high (Yahoo `regularMarketDayHigh`) defines the first hurdle; acceptance above it would signal the Asia dip was a liquidity flush rather than a structural breakdown.

## Fundamental Analysis

Gold's fundamental setup remains a **three-way tension**: (1) geopolitical haven demand from US–Iran escalation, (2) **energy-led inflation** via oil's Monday surge through Middle East supply anxiety, and (3) **monetary policy repricing** as markets weigh whether the Fed must hike again to defend its inflation target.

The Star (July 13, 2026) reported traders lifting September Fed hike odds to roughly **72%** from ~63% last week — a meaningful shift that helps explain why gold slipped **more than 1%** even as conflict headlines worsened. CNBC TV18 summarized the cross-asset logic: higher crude lifts yields and the dollar, raising the opportunity cost of holding bullion. That channel has dominated the **gold price forecast today** narrative more than pure safe-haven buying.

The next scheduled catalyst is **US CPI for June, due July 14, 2026 (8:30 a.m. ET)** — followed by PPI on July 15. Hot prints would likely reinforce the hawkish USD/yields path that pressured gold this session; softer data could allow a relief rally toward **$4,110+** if oil simultaneously eases.
